CS1200: What did I learn from this class?

Resume + Cover Letter

During CS1200, we looked through potential mock internships and created a resume listing our achievements and skills and wrote a cover letter based off of the internship that we found online. I found this assignment useful since I learned that employers actually only scan your resume for about a minute before moving onto someone else's resume. In addition, I was also able to obtain feedback from my peers regarding how to better present myself to employers from the PowerPoint presentation shown in class. I was also reassured that it is never too early to be looking for potential jobs and internships.

Time Management + Prioritization

When starting freshman year, I had a lot of trouble managing my time between friends, family, and schoolwork. Even though I still have a lot of trouble with this now, I believe the Time Management assignment we did in class is useful since it allowed me to get in the habit of reading through the syllabus for each of my classes and seeing what is due when so I can prioritize accordingly. Not only did I figure out how many hours I spend on each activity throughout my day, but it also allowed me to update my Google Calendar accordingly so I would know when to switch from one activity to another.

Failure, Creativity, + Design Thinking

Even though this was a recent topic we learned about in class, I believe this is one of the most meaningful concepts we covered since design thinking can really be applied in the past, present, and future. Furthermore, since I am a Computer Science and Design major, many of my classes involve learning about specific step-by-step approaches to projects using design thinking. Design thinmking involves the past through taking into account what did not work out for your project, the present through what the creator can implement now, and the future through describing what goals the creator has for their project in the future.
